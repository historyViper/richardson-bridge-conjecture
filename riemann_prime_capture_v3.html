Catalan's constant G ≈ 0.9160 is the exact balance point of the C1/C2 two-lane Euler product. Every prime coprime to 30 is assigned χ=+1 (C1, pushes product up) or χ=−1 (C2, pulls product down) by its mod-4 residue. The product converges to G because the mod-30 mirror symmetry — gcd(N−r, N) = gcd(r, N) — pairs every C1 prime with a C2 mirror, preventing either lane from dominating. The Mertens function M(x) is the running score of these ±1 signs. If either lane dominated, M(x) would grow without bound, violating the Riemann Hypothesis. G being finite is the RH in number-theoretic language.
